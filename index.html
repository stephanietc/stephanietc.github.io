About Me

Hi! My name is Stephanie. I'm a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering and the Collaborative Program in Neuroscience at the University of Toronto. I work at the Bloorview Research Institute, creating and evaluating fun, home-based rehabilitation therapies to improve motor control in children with cerebral palsy. To design these interventions, I am merging principles in music-supported rehabilitation and video game therapy. My advisors are Dr. Joyce Chen and Dr. Elaine Biddiss.

Some Past Projects

Physiological Models of Impaired Hearing (2012 - 2014)

  • With Dr. Ian Bruce, I used computational models of the auditory brainstem and midbrain to see how sound localization cues are processed in impaired and aided hearing. We found that linked bilateral hearing aids perform best. You can read more in my thesis.

Biomusic (2010)

  • With Dr. Elaine Biddiss and Elizabeth Han, I evaluated an auditory interface to help caregivers detect anxiety in children with communication difficulties. We mapped physiological indicators of anxiety to sound, creating lively "biomusic". You can find out more at the PEARL Lab website.
