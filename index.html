About Me

Hi! My name is Stephanie. I am a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering and the Collaborative Program in Neuroscience at the University of Toronto. I am interested in using engineering approaches to study and facilitate communication, and to harness people's interest in music for health management and rehabilitation.

Currently, I am working with Dr. Deryk Beal to study the development of speech-motor control, a complex system that allows us to communicate through the quick coordination of many muscles. This research is important to understand the typical development of speech. The knowledge could also contribute to the development of rehabilitation interventions for people with speech disorders.

Science communication is important to me, so I also run a website called All About Kids Research with Karolina Urban. It is dedicated to sharing knowledge about paediatric health research in a meaningful and accessible format.

Past Projects

Music-Based Rehabilitation through Video Games

With Dr. Elaine Biddiss and Dr. Joyce Chen, I designed video games to allow children with cerebral palsy to access music therapy from home. By pairing motion tracking technology with music-supported rehabilitation therapy techniques, these therapies may enhance motor learning in a fun, engaging way.

Find out more

Sound Classification for Mixed-Reality Therapy Games

I used machine learning to detect and classify musical instruments from audio mixtures. In mixed-reality video games for motor therapy, these algorithms will complement motion tracking to detect and reward sounds associated with fine movements of music-making. These movements may be missed by motion tracking alone.

Find out more

Physiological Models of Impaired Sound Localization

Sound localization is important for everyday listening. With Dr. Ian Bruce at McMaster University, I used computational models of the auditory brainstem and midbrain to see how sound localization cues are processed in impaired and aided hearing.

Find out more

Biomusic: Listening to Anxiety

With Dr. Elaine Biddiss and Elizabeth Han, I evaluated an auditory interface to help caregivers detect anxiety in children with communication difficulties. We mapped physiological indicators of anxiety to sounds, creating lively "biomusic" that lets you hear anxious feelings in musical changes.
