About Me

Hi! My name is Stephanie. I'm a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering and the Collaborative Program in Neuroscience at the University of Toronto. At the Bloorview Research Institute, I create and evaluate new interventions to improve motor control in children with cerebral palsy through music-supported therapy and video gaming.

My advisors are Dr. Joyce Chen and Dr. Elaine Biddiss.

Current Project

Movement through Music (2015 - present)

music-supported therapy video games for motor rehabilitation of children with cerebral palsy

Childhood cerebral palsy is a motor disorder which often impacts upper limb movements required for activity and play. New home-based therapies are needed to target motor control with sufficient intensity and engagement for clinical outcomes.

I am developing MusicMaster, a home-based adjunct to conventional therapyDesigned to merge promising directions in interactive computer play and music-supported rehabilitation, MusicMaster may enhance motor learning by increasing engagement and using auditory-motor coupling to target upper extremity control.

Past Projects

Musical Instrument Detection (2014 - 2015)

I used machine learning algorithms to classify musical instrument from audio mixtures recorded with the Microsoft Kinect. In mixed-reality video games for rehabilitation, these algorithms can complement motion tracking to detect and reward fine hand movements, which may be missed by the Kinect.

Find out more

Physiological Models of Impaired Sound Localization (2012 - 2014)

With Dr. Ian Bruce, I used computational neural models to see how sound localization cues are processed in impaired and aided hearing. We found that linked bilateral hearing aids perform best to address impairment.

Find out more

Biomusic: Listening to Anxiety (2011)

With Dr. Elaine Biddiss and Elizabeth Han, I evaluated an auditory interface to help caregivers detect anxiety in children with communication difficulties. We mapped physiological indicators of anxiety to sound, creating lively "biomusic".
