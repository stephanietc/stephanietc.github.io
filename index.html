About Me

Hi! My name is Stephanie. I'm a PhD student in Biomedical Engineering and the Collaborative Program in Neuroscience at the University of Toronto. I work at the Bloorview Research Institute, creating and evaluating new interventions to improve motor control in children with cerebral palsy through music-supported therapy and video gaming.

My advisors are Dr. Joyce Chen and Dr. Elaine Biddiss.

Projects

Movement through Music (2015 - present)

I am working with Dr. Joyce Chen and Dr. Elaine Biddiss to develop new, low-cost, home based interventions targeting motor control in children with cerebral palsy. To do this, I merge recent directions in music-supported rehabilitation and video game therapy. Stay tuned for more!

Physiological Models of Impaired Hearing (2012 - 2014)

With Dr. Ian Bruce, I used computational neural models to see how sound localization cues are processed in impaired and aided hearing. We found that linked bilateral hearing aids perform best. You can read more in my Master's thesis.

Biomusic: Listening to Anxiety (2011)

With Dr. Elaine Biddiss and Elizabeth Han, I evaluated an auditory interface to help caregivers detect anxiety in children with communication difficulties. We mapped physiological indicators of anxiety to sound, creating lively "biomusic". You can find out more at the PEARL Lab website.
